Simulation of 2-Dimensional Heat Conduction using Physics-Informed Neural Networks

My thesis involves using PyTorch to simulate heat conduction in a 2-dimensional space. I am implementing Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) to efficiently model the heat transfer processes. This approach combines the accuracy of physics-based models with the flexibility and scalability of neural networks.

This thesis aims to demonstrate how PINNs can be trained to predict temperature distributions in complex geometries without relying on traditional mesh-based methods. By leveraging PyTorch's computational graph and automatic differentiation capabilities, I am exploring how neural networks can be adapted to enforce physical constraints directly into the learning process.

The project involves preprocessing data, designing the neural network architecture, training models on GPU-enabled systems, and validating results against analytical or experimental data. It also includes optimizing hyperparameters to enhance model accuracy and performance.
